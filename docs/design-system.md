The overall feel: Editorial business document, not SaaS marketing page. Think The Economist or a well-designed McKinsey PDF — confident, quiet, content-forward. The design gets out of the way so the writing can do the work. Nothing bounces, nothing gradients, nothing glows.
Color palette. Two blues doing all the heavy lifting.

Deep navy #1F3A5F — headers, section titles, numerals, the trust color
Ice blue #D5E8F0 — callout backgrounds, accent blocks, the "pay attention here" color
Light gray-blue #F3F6F9 — card backgrounds, subtle section grouping
Near-black #1A1A1A — body text (not pure black — pure black is harsh on screens and prints muddy)
Muted gray #555555 — footer, tertiary info
Border gray #CCCCCC — thin table borders

The navy-and-ice-blue pairing is the thing that makes it feel like ArcticBlue and not a generic corporate doc. That's the identity anchor — everything else is servant to it.
Typography. Arial everywhere. Sounds boring; it's deliberate. Arial renders identically on every machine, survives Google Docs conversion, and doesn't announce itself. Size hierarchy: big title (14pt bold navy), section headers (10pt bold navy, ALL CAPS), sub-heads inside cards (10pt bold navy, normal case), body (9pt regular near-black), footer (9pt muted gray). The two moves that matter: section headers are in small caps + navy (editorial signal), and card titles are bold navy at the same size as body (prevents visual shouting).
Layout grammar. Everything is tables. Header band is a 2-column table with navy fill. Callout is a single-cell table with ice-blue fill. "What we do" is 3 columns, light-gray fill. "How it works" is 2 columns with a navy numeral box on the left. Tiers are 3 columns (tag, description, price). No floating boxes, no decorative graphics, no icons. The table-based rhythm is what makes it feel like a proper document instead of a web page exported to Word.
Spacing discipline. Tight. 0.5-inch margins, 80-unit cell padding (top/bottom), 140-unit cell padding (left/right). Paragraph spacing is 20 before / 20 after — about half of Word's default. This is what lets the whole thing fit on one page without feeling crammed. Density is a feature, not a bug — a one-pager that needs two pages is a two-pager.
Punctuation signature. Em dashes with spaces around them — like this — not the tight em-dashes some style guides prefer. Middle dots (·) for separators in the footer and tagline. Smart quotes, never straight. No Oxford commas in short phrases, yes in lists of three or more.
Anti-patterns — things the design explicitly avoids. No drop shadows. No rounded corners above 2px. No gradients. No icons (especially no emoji icons). No full-width color bands beyond the navy header. No sans-serif-that-isn't-Arial like Inter or Poppins — those read as "AI startup deck" and ArcticBlue is positioning as the adult in the room. No images of diverse teams in conference rooms. No abstract geometric shapes.
