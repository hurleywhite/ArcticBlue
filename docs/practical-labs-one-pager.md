# ArcticMind · AI Practical Labs — One-Pager

## The problem

Your team has AI tools, but they're barely using them.

You've made the investment in AI. But the tools change weekly, the techniques shift daily, and most of your team is still figuring out where to start, let alone do anything useful with what they have. Giving people access to AI doesn't make them good at it. That takes practice — the kind you can't get from a one-time workshop or a self-paced course.

**AI Practical Labs are monthly, hands-on working sessions where your team actually gets good at using AI, not just aware of it.**

## Sound familiar?

- **You've rolled out AI tools and nobody's using them.** You're paying for Copilot, ChatGPT, and a handful of other tools. Daily usage is still in the single digits. People know AI exists. That's not the same as knowing how to use it.
- **One-off training wears off fast.** A workshop gets people excited for a week. Then things go back to normal. And whatever you taught last quarter is already out of date.
- **Everything moves too fast to keep up.** New models, new features, new use cases every week. Your team doesn't know what to focus on, so they don't focus on any of it.
- **Leadership wants to see AI actually working.** Execs don't care that people attended a training. They want to know AI is making a difference.

## How it works

### 01 · Monthly live session built around your team

A group of up to 20 members of your team works through a real business problem using current AI tools. This isn't just slides and lectures. Everyone's doing the work, live, together.

### 02 · New challenge every month to always stay current

Each session covers different tools and techniques based on what's actually useful right now — strategy, ops, comms, analysis — and your team builds range instead of going deep on one thing that'll be obsolete next quarter.

### 03 · Run by people who do this for a living

Our team runs enterprise AI enablement programs every day. These sessions aren't vendor demos or product walkthroughs — they're actual working sessions led by expert practitioners.

### 04 · Snowball effect

Each month builds on the last. Over time, your team develops a shared vocabulary, a habit of experimenting, and the confidence to figure things out on their own.

## What your team walks away with

- **Real fluency.** People who can actually use AI on real work, not just talk about it.
- **Always current.** Hands-on time with whatever tools and techniques matter this month, not six months ago.
- **Momentum.** A monthly rhythm that keeps AI from falling off the radar.

## What's included (per engagement)

- One 90-minute live session per month
- Challenge built around your team's actual work
- Materials and follow-up resources
- ArcticMind facilitation team included
- Up to 20 participants — cancel anytime

## What this means for the app

The app is the between-session home for Practical Labs buyers. The dashboard must open with **this month's Lab** — title, challenge, date, pre-work, RSVP, post-session artifacts. Everything else (Canvas, Analyzer, Learning Hub, Use Cases, Tools, Resources) is **support material for the Lab**. The monthly rhythm is the product; the app makes the snowball visible.
